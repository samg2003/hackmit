One of the many consequences of climate change and global warming is the melting of our ice caps. As all of that ice melts, the newly-freed liquid water enters our ocean and changes our sea levels. Now rising sea levels threatens many coastal environments. Unless we make drastic and immediate changes to the current rate of climate change, all of our most precious sea-side cities, towns, and ecosystems will be lost to the ocean.

About The Project

This website was created as a submission for HackMIT 2021. The goal is to use data analysis to show the impact of climate change to public individuals and hopefully convince more people to take action. It was created using GMSL dataset, Open Elevation API, and statsmodels library.

We have used Open Elevation API for checking the elevation of the coordinates that the user provided. Subsequently, we used GMSL dataset and ran vector autoregression to predict when the global mean sea level would rise over the given coordinate elevation.
